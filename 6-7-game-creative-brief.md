## 1. Brand Overview

### 1.1 Brand Essence

**"Your World is Six-Seven. Let's Make It AWESOME."**

The 6-7 Game transforms the mundane "six out of seven" reality of daily life into an adventure worth playing. We believe every child's world ‚Äî from their bedroom to the universe ‚Äî can level up through learning, play, and creative mastery.

The name "6-7" comes from the universal experience of rating your current state as "six out of seven" ‚Äî not terrible, but not amazing. The game exists to help kids (and families) break through that ceiling and reach their full potential.

## 2. Visual Identity

### 2.1 Visual Evolution System

The 6-7 Game's signature visual mechanic is **progressive graphics evolution**:

| Level Range | Visual Style | Description |
|-------------|--------------|-------------|
| 1-3 | 8-bit Pixel Art | Gritty, nostalgic, intentionally "boring" starting point |
| 4-6 | 16-bit Pixel Art | Colors pop, details emerge, world feels more alive |
| 7-9 | 32-bit Graphics | Smooth animations, richer environments |
| 10-11 | HD Cartoon | Full cartoon-quality characters and worlds |

**Key Insight:** This visual progression creates tangible, visible proof of achievement. Kids can literally *see* themselves leveling up.

## 4. UI/UX Guidelines

### 4.1 Design Principles

1. **Fun First, Learning Hidden**
   - Never lead with "educational" ‚Äî lead with gameplay
   - Standards codes and learning metrics are for parents, not kids
   - Celebrate achievements with visual fanfare

2. **Progressive Disclosure**
   - Start simple, reveal complexity as players level up
   - New features unlock, keeping the experience fresh
   - Don't overwhelm new users

3. **Immediate Feedback**
   - Every action has visible, satisfying feedback
   - Haptics on Watch for answers (correct = celebration, wrong = gentle nudge)
   - Mollars animate when earned

4. **Accessible by Default**
   - High contrast for readability
   - Large touch targets (especially on Watch)
   - Clear visual hierarchy

### 4.2 Parent App UI

#### Dashboard
- **Header:** Family name, total Mollars earned today
- **Child Cards:** Each child shows name, Mollar balance, today's activity
- **Quick Actions:** Start Battle, View Progress, Settings

#### Child Detail View
- **Learning Progress:** Visual skill tree or grid showing mastered standards
- **Activity Log:** Recent questions, battles, Mollars earned
- **Settings:** Grade level (editable), notification preferences

#### Battle Configuration
- Clean, card-based UI for settings
- Sliders or segmented controls for options (questions, time, subject)
- Preview of what each setting means

### 4.3 Watch App UI

#### Main Menu (3 Options)
Large, tappable cards:
1. **üìä My Dashboard** ‚Äî See your stats
2. **ü™ô Earn Mollars** ‚Äî Practice mode

#### Quiz Interface

## 5. Marketing Strategy

### 5.1 Target Audiences

#### Primary: Parents (Decision Makers)
- **Demographics:** Parents of kids ages 7-11, tech-savvy, Apple ecosystem
- **Pain Points:** Guilt about screen time, desire for educational value, sibling rivalry
- **Messaging Focus:** "Screen time that counts," standards alignment, parent dashboard

#### Secondary: Kids (Users)
- **Demographics:** Ages 7-11 (Grades 2-5), competitive, creative
- **Pain Points:** Boring educational apps, unfair competition with older siblings
- **Messaging Focus:** Visual evolution, fair battles, Mollars economy

#### Tertiary: Educators
- **Demographics:** Teachers, tutors, curriculum specialists
- **Pain Points:** Engagement, standards alignment, progress tracking
- **Messaging Focus:** B.E.S.T. alignment, learning components, classroom potential

### 5.2 Channel Strategy

| Channel | Purpose | Content Type |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| **Website** | Conversion, waitlist | Product showcase, demo, testimonials |
| **Instagram** | Awareness, community | Visual evolution GIFs, kid testimonials, parent tips |
| **TikTok** | Viral awareness | Kids playing, before/after visuals, family battles |
| **Facebook** | Parent targeting | Longer-form content, group community |
| **YouTube** | Education, trust | Gameplay videos, parent tutorials, founder story |
| **Email** | Nurture, updates | Waitlist updates, launch announcements |

### 5.3 Content Themes

#### Theme 1: The Visual Glow-Up
Show the transformation from 8-bit to HD. Before/after style content.
- "Day 1 vs. Day 30"
- Timelapse of world evolution
- Side-by-side character comparisons

#### Theme 2: Fair Family Battles
Capture authentic family competition moments.
- Sibling rivalries (friendly)
- Parent vs. kid battles
- The moment of victory

#### Theme 3: Sneaky Learning
Position education as the hidden superpower.
- "He doesn't know he's doing homework"
- "She learned fractions... by accident"
- Parents reacting to progress reports

#### Theme 4: The Mollars Economy
Make the currency feel real and valuable.
- Kids counting their Mollars
- Strategies for earning more
- What Mollars unlock

### 5.4 Launch Campaign Concept

**Campaign Name:** "Level Up Your Family"

**Phases:**
1. **Teaser (2 weeks before):** "Something's coming to make your world AWESOME"
2. **Launch:** Full reveal, waitlist opens, demo available

**Key Visuals:**
